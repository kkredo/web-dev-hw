How to Cast a Fly Fishing Rod


Fly fishing is one of most popular, yet challenging mediums to catch trout in your local lake or stream. Mastering the proper technique to cast a fly with a fly rod will take some time, but with these easy steps you'll be on the water catching trout in no time.


Materials Needed

  • Fly Rod
  • Reel
  • Line
  • Leader
  • Tippet
  • Clear area to cast

    • The Overhead Cast

    The overhead cast is the most simple and highly used style of casting.

    To perform the overhead cast, you must know how to "load" the rod, which means finding and creating the tension in the line and rod to cast the fly.


    Step One

    Holding the Rod Properly

  • Hold the cork handle with your thumb on top
  • Have the butt of the rod in line with your forearm
  • Make sure the reel is facing the ground

    • Step Two

    Preparing the Line

  • Strip out about 10 ft. of line from the reel
  • Make sure line is untangled and you are sqaure with your target

    • Step Three

    Performing the Cast

  • Keep the rod tip low the water and in one fluid movement, raise the rod tip by only bending your elbow
  • Keep your wrist fixed
  • This creates tension , loading the rod
  • As a beginner, look behind you and watch for the loop created by the line to unfold
  • When the loop is unfolded and the line is straight, lower the rod tip in one motion
  • Allowing loop to straighten loads the rod and propels fly forward when the tip is lowered
